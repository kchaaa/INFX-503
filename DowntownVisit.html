Downtown Seattle:

The Most Iconic Scene of Seattle

Downtown Seattle is probably what people imagine when they think of Seattle. They think about Pike's Place Market, the Space Needle, Century Link Field, and Safeco Stadium. I personally believe there is more to Downtown Seattle than just those 4 places. This is probably the part of Seattle that will make sure you will never be bored.


Near Pike's Place, there is the Seattle Art Museum (SAM). The museum has very interesting exhibits. If you go towards the pier, you will see Pier 57 where there are amazing sights such as the Seattle Great Wheel and the Seattle Aquarium.


If you don't know the picture above, it's the Columbia Center. The Columbia Center is the tallest skyscraper in Seattle that makes the Space Needle look like an ant! It helps that it is much cheaper and less of a wait. It is probably my favorite place in Downtown. Nearby the Columbia Center, there is the Seattle Public Library. The architecture alone will amaze you. The library is super tall meaning it has a very wide selection.


Close to the Space Needle, there is many fun places to visit such as an amazing museum known as the EMP ; a beautiful sight at the Glass museum; a science museum with amazing exhibits known as Pacific Place; and a circular foundtain to go especially during the Spring/Summer.


Last, but not least, you cannot miss the places surrounding the stadium. Pioneer Square has a very historical feeling to it and is close to a lot of sports bars. And a 5 minute walk away is the International District. This is the home of Asian culture in Seattle. With places like Chinatown and Little Vietnam inside the district, you can see the variety of Asian culture and eat a lot of amazing Asian food.
